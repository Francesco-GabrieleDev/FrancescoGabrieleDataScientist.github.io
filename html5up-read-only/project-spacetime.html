Possibly Broke Spacetime

A hands-on story about the experiment that warped the field and taught me more about uncertainty than any textbook.

What started as a playful side project became a series of field tests across three continents. I built an array of synchronized sensors, then guided them through micro-second pulses until one of the pulses came back distorted. The vivid blue streaks from the readings inspired the suspicion that a localized frame had shifted, which turned out to be the moment I learned how resilient the math really is.

The experiment

Two telescopes, a fiber optic cable loop, and a machine learning model that predicted jitter before it happened. We tracked the way light curved inside the pulse and compared it to simulations; when reality differed, we dove deeper.

Key takeaways

Precision is more than hardware—it is workflow. The work taught me how to build reliable data pipelines and to stay calm when the unexpected curveball arrives, trusting the team and the math that never lied.
