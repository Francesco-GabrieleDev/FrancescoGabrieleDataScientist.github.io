Domain 02 — Geospatial

Fine-Grained Mobility
Prediction with GNNs

Predicting human mobility at the parcel level using graph neural networks to proxy retail demand.

Fine-Grained Mobility Prediction for Retail Demand using Graph Neural Networks

Problem Addressed
Classical gravity-based mobility models break down at fine resolutions. This project predicts incoming human mobility at the parcel level — specifically for individual gas station sites.

Why It Matters
Human mobility pings within station canopies show a ~0.7 correlation with actual sales, making mobility a strong proxy for demand.

Model Performance

  • Mean absolute error of ~19 pings per station.
  • Graph-based models outperformed non-graph baselines by explicitly modeling spatial context and local competition.

(Metrics evaluated at station level across ~2.8k sites)

Business Impact

  • Established a framework for white-space analysis to support new site placement.
  • Enabled site-level demand estimation without direct access to sales labels.
  • Captured competitive spillovers and spatial dependencies missed by classical mobility models.

POI Segmentation from
Satellite Imagery

Automated polygon extraction for points of interest using YOLOv11 — enabling large-scale geometry harvesting at minimal cost.

POI Geometries Retrieval with Image Segmentation from Satellite Imagery

Problem Addressed
Developed an automated framework to extract polygon geometries of points of interest from satellite imagery using a YOLOv11 segmentation model.

Why It Matters
The extracted geometries enabled accurate measurement of human activity within each POI area.

Model Performance
mAP@50–90: 0.57 · mAP@50: 0.96

Business Impact

  • Eliminated the need for third-party manual annotation, reducing operational costs.
  • Enabled large-scale geometry extraction (10k+ POIs processed in ~1 hour).
  • Learned compact POI representations reusable for downstream modeling tasks.

Domain 01 — Renewable Energy

Map-Based Renewable
Energy Platform

A spatially-aware simulation platform for PV and wind feasibility analysis — no coding required.

Map-Based Renewable Energy Simulation Platform (in development)

Problem Addressed
Energy feasibility and performance analyses for photovoltaic and wind assets are often detached from spatial context and require technical expertise.

Solution
Developing a map-centric web platform that allows users to place, configure, and simulate PV systems and wind turbines directly on an interactive map, with asset-level dashboards generated automatically from backend simulations.

Why It Matters
Enables no-code, spatially explicit exploration of renewable energy scenarios, improving interpretability and decision-making at both single-asset and territorial scale.

Programming Tools
Python (geospatial stack, pvlib, wind turbine models), React, Leaflet, PostgreSQL/PostGIS, Docker.
